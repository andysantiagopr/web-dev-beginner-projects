Lasagna Recipe

Ingredients

For the Meat Sauce (Ragù)

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 medium carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 1 lb (450 g) ground beef (or half beef, half pork)
  • 1 can (28 oz / 800 g) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup (240 ml) beef broth or red wine
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil (or a few fresh leaves)
  • Salt & pepper to taste

For the Ricotta Layer

  • 15 oz (425 g) ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh parsley (optional)

Other

  • 12 lasagna noodles (traditional or oven-ready)
  • 3 cups (300 g) shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan (extra for topping)

Steps:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat. Add onion, garlic, carrot, and celery. Sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the ground beef (or beef and pork mix). Cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon. Drain excess fat if needed.
  3. Stir in tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, beef broth or red wine, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Reduce heat and simmer for 30–40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until sauce thickens.
  4. In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese, egg, Parmesan, and parsley. Mix until smooth and set aside.
  5. If using traditional noodles, cook them in salted boiling water until al dente. Drain and lay on parchment paper to prevent sticking. (Skip if using oven-ready noodles.)
  6. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Spread a thin layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9×13 inch baking dish.
  7. Layer noodles over the sauce. Spread some ricotta mixture on the noodles, add more meat sauce, then sprinkle mozzarella.
  8. Repeat layers (noodles → ricotta → sauce → mozzarella) until ingredients are used, finishing with sauce and mozzarella on top. Sprinkle extra Parmesan.
  9. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for another 20 minutes until bubbly and golden on top.
  10. Remove from oven and let rest for 10–15 minutes before slicing and serving.
